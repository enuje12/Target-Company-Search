# Methodology

1. I selected Hyderabad as the target city because it has a dense concentration of specialty biotech manufacturers that match DeepThought’s Federer ICP. I also personally visited Genome Valley during my college’s experiential learning program, which gave me first-hand exposure to the biotech ecosystem there. From there, I focused on companies that appeared to be genuine manufacturers rather than traders, distributors, CROs, labs, or pure service businesses. This helped me build a list of companies that actually produce physical products and show meaningful technical depth.

2. I used Perplexity and Claude to find candidate companies and gather initial information because these AI research assistants since both tools help surface verified public sources and evidence that can be manually checked, which makes the research process faster and easier to verify than doing every step from scratch. After that, I manually verified each company myself using company websites, LinkedIn pages, leadership profiles, and other public sources such as industry directories and company reference pages to make sure the final list was accurate and evidence-based.

3. For each company, I checked to confirm the product category, city presence, decision-maker background, and signs of differentiation or growth. I then scored each company against the six assignment criteria: manufacturer, India-based, differentiated, technical decision-maker, growing sector, and active growth signals. Each score was backed by one line of evidence so the output remained transparent and easy to audit.

4. To reduce false positives and hallucination risk, I applied strict filters before final inclusion. Companies were excluded if they were primarily traders, distributors, service-only firms, generic pharma, PE-controlled, above the revenue threshold, or had no meaningful evidence of recent activity. Borderline companies were kept only when there was enough evidence to justify further review. The final CSV therefore reflects both qualification judgment and disqualification reasoning, not just a list of names.

5. Python with pandas was used to clean and organize the final CSV. The script helped remove duplicate entries, sort the companies by band, and export the file in a proper spreadsheet-friendly format. The actual company research and scoring were done manually using verified public sources.
